Golden is the hour when the sky grows shy, bidding but a fleeting farewell to its foremost source of luster. 

Golden are the expectant eyes chasing the rays of sunshine once more, for goodbye was whispered with no promise of hello. 

Golden is the sun reflecting on a petal’s flower. Lost in a meadow with blossoms waking in the light it will, tell a story of a bee dancing on the hill. 

Golden is the heart looking back on memories. Under summer’s heat it shall, shine through reddening cheeks. 

Golden is the world turning technicolor, although what was once expectant is now closed forever. For even the blind can see, the rays seering through their eyes with a youthful glee. Indeed, no vision yet clarity only can tell, the tales of a colourful child far away in a dell. 

Golden is the man who shared with his grandchildren, the essence of joy that is no longer foreign. 

Golden is the soul that wants to meet again, the person to whom you owe, the breezy spirit you so often refrain.
